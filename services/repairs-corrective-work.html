Repairs & Corrective Work

Code corrections, damage repairs, and deferred maintenance handled completely and correctly — not patched over and sent back to cause problems later.

Done Right the First Time

Too many repair jobs get the band-aid treatment — a quick patch that hides the problem without actually fixing it. JNS Construction Services LLC approaches repair and corrective work the same way we approach new construction: identify the actual cause, define the full scope of the fix, and execute it with the right materials and the right methods.

Whether you're dealing with storm damage, a moisture intrusion that's been spreading behind walls, code violations flagged during a sale inspection, or a backlog of deferred maintenance, we provide a written assessment, a clear proposal, and work that holds up under scrutiny — including permit inspections where required.

  • Root-cause assessment, not just surface patching
  • Written scope so you know exactly what's being addressed and why
  • Permits pulled where required — we don't cut corners on code
  • Work performed by licensed, field-supervised tradespeople
  • Documentation provided for insurance claims, resale, or permit close-out

Why Proper Corrective Work Matters

Repairs done without the right scope or permitting create compounding problems. Unpermitted structural repairs can surface during a home sale inspection, forcing rework at the worst possible time. Moisture damage that's covered rather than addressed keeps spreading. Code violations that aren't properly corrected may void insurance claims or create liability.

When JNS Construction Services LLC performs corrective work, we document everything: the condition found, the work performed, the materials used, and any relevant permit records. This protects your investment and provides a defensible record for insurance adjusters, future buyers, and code enforcement.

  • Licensed contractor repairs carry weight with insurance adjusters and inspectors
  • Permitted repairs are documented in county records — no ambiguity at point of sale
  • Properly scoped work prevents the same problem from reappearing six months later
  • Written documentation provided for your records

Our Process

1

Assessment & Documentation

We inspect the affected area, identify the source and extent of the issue, and document existing conditions — including photographs where relevant for insurance or permit purposes.

2

Written Scope & Proposal

We define exactly what needs to be removed, replaced, corrected, or reinforced — with itemized pricing and a clear explanation of what the work addresses and why. No guesswork billing.

3

Permitting Where Required

If the scope requires a permit, we apply before work begins. We don't skip permitting steps to move faster — unpermitted corrective work defeats the purpose of doing it right.

4

Corrective Work Execution

Work is performed in the correct sequence — abatement before repair, structure before finish — with experienced tradespeople under field supervision. Adjacent undamaged areas are protected.

5

Inspection & Sign-Off

Permitted work is inspected and signed off by the county. All work is walked with you upon completion. We provide documentation of the work performed and any final inspection records.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do repair and corrective work projects sometimes require a permit?

Permits are required when structural elements, load-bearing components, or systems like plumbing or electrical are affected. Permitted repairs are documented in county records — which protects you during insurance claims, resale inspections, and code enforcement inquiries. We pull permits wherever required, no exceptions.

Can you correct work done by a previous contractor?

Yes. We assess the existing conditions, identify what was done incorrectly or left incomplete, and develop a written corrective scope. If the original work violates code, we address the violation through the proper permit process. We’ll be upfront about what we’re inheriting and what it means for cost and timeline.

How do you handle water damage repairs when mold is present?

Our scope covers structural and finish material removal and reinstallation. If mold remediation is involved, we coordinate with a certified remediation contractor before proceeding with repairs. We don’t reinstall over active mold — remediation happens first, then we bring the structure back correctly.

Do you provide documentation for insurance claims?

Yes. We document existing conditions with photos before work begins, maintain records of all work performed and materials used, and retain permit records where applicable. A complete close-out package is provided at project completion for your records and insurance purposes.
